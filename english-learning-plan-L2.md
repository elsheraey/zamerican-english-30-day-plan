## Realistic Expectations After Completing This Plan

### You'll reach: **A2+ to B1** (Pre-Intermediate to Intermediate)

| Skill | Where You'll Be | CEFR |
|-------|-----------------|------|
| **Listening** | Can follow slow-to-medium conversations, understand news with some pausing | A2+ |
| **Speaking** | Can handle daily situations (airport, shopping, weather, opinions) | A2+ |
| **Reading** | Can read short stories and news articles with some dictionary help | B1 |
| **Writing** | Can write basic paragraphs and short essays | A2 |
| **Grammar** | Solid grasp of tenses, modals, gerunds/infinitives, comparatives | B1 |
| **Pronunciation** | Noticeably better — can distinguish British vs American sounds | B1 |
| **Vocabulary** | ~1,500-2,000 active words | A2-B1 |

### What you CAN do after this plan

- Introduce yourself and hold basic conversations
- Understand the gist of English YouTube videos (with subtitles)
- Read simple English articles and stories
- Write short emails or messages
- Order food, ask directions, handle travel situations
- Start thinking in English for simple thoughts

### What you CAN'T do yet

- Watch movies/series without subtitles comfortably
- Have deep or fast-paced conversations
- Write professional emails or essays
- Pass IELTS/TOEFL (you'd need Level 3 + dedicated prep)
- Understand native slang, humor, or rapid speech
