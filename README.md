# 30-Day English Learning Plan (Level 2)

A complete, day-by-day English study plan built around the free
[**ZAmericanEnglish**](https://www.youtube.com/@ZAmericanEnglish) courses by Ibrahim Adel,
paired with a daily Duolingo habit. Thirty days, sixty-three videos, eight playlists, all
sequenced so nothing gets left behind.

It takes you from late-beginner to comfortable pre-intermediate (**A2+ to B1**): real
conversations, story-based listening, news comprehension, and the start of *thinking* in
English instead of translating.

## What this is

This started as a small experiment: could an AI turn a sprawling YouTube playlist into a
structured, motivating, finish-able study plan for someone who actually wants to learn?

I built it with **Claude** for a friend who was working through the ZAmericanEnglish
Level 2 courses and wanted a clear map instead of a wall of videos. I picked up most of my
own English by ear rather than from any structured course, so I'm not the right person to
hand-build a syllabus. Having AI do it meant my friend got a real plan, properly ordered
and paced, instead of my vague "just watch a lot and you'll get it."

Claude did the heavy lifting: auditing every playlist, ordering the 63 videos into a
coherent 30-day arc, balancing the daily load, and writing two very different versions of
the same plan (a no-nonsense reference table and a warm, day-by-day coaching guide, in both
English and Egyptian Arabic).

I'm sharing it as a friendly add-on to my portfolio, partly to show what Claude can do
when pointed at "make a genuinely useful learning resource," and partly because a good
study plan is the kind of thing that's nicer when it's not locked in one person's chat
history. If it helps you (or someone you're rooting for) get through Level 2, that's the
whole point. 🙂

## The 30-day shape

- **Week 1:** Grammar foundation, plus start thinking in English
- **Week 2:** Phonetics, conversation, and "Learn Like Westerners"
- **Week 3:** Stories and listening (where it starts clicking)
- **Week 4:** News and immersion, then two days to consolidate and prove it to yourself

About **one hour a day**: a Duolingo lesson in the morning, ZAmericanEnglish videos in
the afternoon, a short review before bed.

## How to use it

1. Pick your version: tables ([`-L2.md`](english-learning-plan-L2.md)) if you just want
   the schedule, or the guide ([`-guide.md`](english-learning-plan-L2-guide.md) /
   [Arabic](english-learning-plan-L2-guide-ar.md)) if you want the daily nudge.
2. Bookmark the eight playlists (linked at the top of the plan).
3. Show up every day. Keep a small vocabulary journal. Don't break the streak.
